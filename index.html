Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about business registration, compliance, and our services.

Which business structure is right for me? (e.g., LLP vs. Pvt Ltd)

Choosing the right structure depends on your goals. A Private Limited (Pvt Ltd) Company is often preferred by startups seeking funding and wanting a scalable structure with limited liability. An LLP (Limited Liability Partnership) offers the flexibility of a partnership with the benefit of limited liability and has fewer compliance burdens, making it ideal for professional firms. A Sole Proprietorship is the simplest to start but offers no liability protection. We can help you decide based on your specific vision, funding needs, and long-term goals.

Is GST registration mandatory for my new business?

GST registration is mandatory if your annual turnover exceeds ₹40 lakhs for goods or ₹20 lakhs for services (with some exceptions for special category states). However, it is also compulsory for businesses involved in e-commerce, interstate trade, or for those who wish to claim input tax credit (ITC). Getting GST registration from the start can make your business appear more credible to clients and suppliers.

What are the annual compliances after registering my company?

After incorporation, a Private Limited Company must adhere to several key annual compliances with the Registrar of Companies (ROC). These typically include:

  • Filing Annual Returns (Form MGT-7/7A)
  • Filing Financial Statements (Form AOC-4)
  • Conducting at least four Board Meetings and one Annual General Meeting (AGM)
  • Maintaining statutory registers and minutes of meetings
  • Filing Income Tax Returns and completing a statutory audit.

We provide comprehensive compliance packages to ensure you never miss a deadline.

Can I register my business with a residential address?

Yes, you can use a residential address as the registered office for your company, provided you have the legal right to use the property for business purposes. You will need to provide a No Objection Certificate (NOC) from the property owner and submit a recent utility bill (like electricity or water bill) as proof of address. This is a common and cost-effective option for many startups and home-based businesses.

What is the difference between a Trademark and a Copyright?

While both protect intellectual property, they cover different things. A Trademark protects brand identity elements like a business name, logo, or slogan that distinguishes your goods or services from others. A Copyright protects original creative works like literature, music, artistic works, software code, and website content. In short, a trademark protects your brand, while a copyright protects your creative output.
